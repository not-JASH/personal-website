Feasibility Study for an Expansion at the Canadian High Arctic Research Station.

Autumn 2017
More Info

A study of renewable energy production technologies and arctic building techniques to determine the viability of building a power-generating expansion to POLAR Knowledge Canada's High Arctic Research Station in Cambridge Bay, Nunavut. This was in collaboration with, and presented to, representatives from POLAR Knowledge Canada and the University of Ottawa's Civil Engineering Department.

Shallow Foundation Design for a Condominium at 231 Cobourg Ave.

December 2017
More Info

A shallow raft-foundation design for a proposed eight-story condomium at 231 Coburg Ave, Ottawa, Ontario. The site at Cobourg Avenue is unique in that beneath a thin layer of sand rests a deep layer of Leda Clay close to the water-table. Leda Clay is known to liquify under pressure when saturated, so increased safety measures were considered in the design.

Waste-to-Energy Plant Design to Service Canadian High Arctic Research Station.

Winter 2018
More Info

A design for a waste-to-energy plant to be built in the High Arctic. This building was designed to reduce Cambridge Bay's ecological footprint while meeting its growing energy needs. With the knowledge gained from the earlier conducted feasibility study, a light-weight building was designed which would sit a few feet above the permafrost on socketed piles. This building also featured sate of the art insulation materials. This report was presented to professors in the University of Ottawa's Civil Engineering Department.

Computer generated training data.

Autumn 2020
More Info

A pipeline to create segmented data in several prominent segmented image formats: YOLO and COCO for example. Virtual rooms were procedurally generated in blender and sequences of images, with corresponding segments for objects and walls, were generated. Three dimensional vertices defining objects in-frame were projected onto the image plane and reduced to only those necessary to express the object's silhouette. Then the segmented data is converted to the desired format. The aim was to determine if a neural network could learn from computer generated graphics.

WaveGAN.

Winter 2021
More Info

An investigation into the temporal variation of entropioc signals, as revealed by time windowing its wavelet transform. A generative adversarial neural network was then developed which sought to lean how the wavelet transform of a signal varied with time and would therefore be able to extrapolate it forward.

Proposals for a Trading AI.

Winter 2022
More Info

An overview of an AI designed to trade Cryptocurrency. Modelling financial markets a stochastic process, the proposed AI implements generative adversarial networks to make reasonable price predictions. Wavelets are also implemented in order to reveal lower frequency trends. Implicit in this design is the cyclical nature of seemingly random events.
